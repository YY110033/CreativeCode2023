Project #1 Translate Human Language into Code





Step 1: Instruction from Etizaz



- Start by selecting 9 cubes anywhere on the canvas in a horizontal line and fill it with the color orange!

- Do the same until you have an orange square where it will be 9x6 cubes (9 top and bottom and 6 left and right)

- Next: on the top of the square; create two other small squares on left and right side of the square shape, and you can add 1 extra cube in the top middle of the shapes. (h:2 - w:3)

- Next: in the middle of the square; add two black cubes with 3 cubes distance from each-other.

- Next: go down 2 rows in the cubes and add one red cube in the middle leaving 1 row empty before the square line.


Step 2: Work with Code/h2>




Reflection

Human Language is sometimes ambiguous, and audiences will have a different understanding. Sometimes, they will come up with the correct answer.

But computer code needs to be clear and precise. If the code is unclear, you will never get the right answer.

Also, humans have a default common sense. Although the writer/speaker didn't say a thing clearly, audiences will complete what the writer/speaker said in their mind.

But the computer doesn't have the common sense. So the computer code must be very clear in every step, even some very simple steps. Otherwise, the computer cannot understand it and cannot process the right result.
